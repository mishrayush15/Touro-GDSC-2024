General behavior practices


Photography Restrictions:


- Sensitive Areas - Photography is restricted in certain sensitive areas, such as military installations and some government buildings.

- Respect for Privacy - It is important to respect people's privacy when taking photographs in public spaces.




Public Behavior and Dress Code:


- Politeness and Respect - Japanese culture places a high value on politeness and respect. Loud or disruptive behavior in public places
is generally frowned upon.

- Dress Code - In more formal settings, conservative and modest clothing is expected. When visiting religious sites, it's advisable to dress modestly.

- Tattoos - While attitudes are changing, tattoos are sometimes associated with organized crime in Japan. Some public places,
such as hot springs (onsen), may have policies against tattooed individuals




Emergency Contact Information:


- Emergency Services - Dial 119 for fire and ambulance services, and 110 for police.

- Embassy/Consulate - Know the contact information for your country's embassy or consulate in case of emergencies or if you lose your passport.

- Local Contacts - Have contact information for local contacts, such as the place you're staying or local friends.
