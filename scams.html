COMMON SCAMS THAT MAY HAPPEN WITH YOU!!!
1.Fake Goods: Be cautious when purchasing branded goods, especially from street vendors or markets. Counterfeit goods are prevalent in tourist areas, and buyers may end up with low-quality or fake items
2.Overpriced Services: Tourists may be charged inflated prices for services such as taxi rides, souvenirs, or food in tourist-heavy areas. Always check prices beforehand and be wary of establishments that don't display prices clearly.
3.Unlicensed Tour Guides: Some individuals may pose as tour guides without proper licenses. It's safer to book tours through reputable agencies or verify the credentials of individual guides before hiring them.
4.Fake Tickets: When purchasing tickets for attractions or events, especially from unofficial sellers or online platforms, there's a risk of buying counterfeit or invalid tickets. Stick to authorized sellers and official websites to avoid this scam.
5.Distraction Theft: Tourists may be targeted by thieves who use distraction techniques, such as asking for directions or spilling something on the victim, to steal belongings. Stay vigilant and keep your belongings secure at all times, especially in crowded places.
6."Friendly" Locals Offering Help: While many locals are genuinely helpful, some may approach tourists offering assistance with directions or recommendations but then expect payment for their help. Politely decline if you're not comfortable, or offer a small tip if you feel the assistance was genuine.
7.Credit Card Fraud: Exercise caution when using credit cards, especially in smaller shops or restaurants. Card skimming devices may be used to steal card information. Stick to reputable establishments and consider using cash for smaller purchases.
8.Rental Damage Scams: When renting bicycles, cars, or other equipment, thoroughly inspect the item for existing damage before use and take photos if necessary. Some rental companies may try to charge tourists for pre-existing damage upon return.
