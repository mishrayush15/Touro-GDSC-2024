Traffic and Vehicle rules

Public Transportation Rules:

Trains and Subways :

- Queueing - Maintain a queue when waiting for trains. Allow passengers to exit before boarding.

- Priority Seats - Priority seats are designated for elderly, disabled, and pregnant passengers. Yield these seats if necessary.

- Quiet Cars - Some trains have designated "quiet cars" where passengers are expected to refrain from talking on the phone or engaging in loud conversations.

Buses :

- Boarding and Exiting - Board buses through the front door and exit through the rear door. Allow elderly, disabled, and those with young children to board and exit first.

- Priority Seats - Similar to trains, buses have priority seats for specific passengers.

- Quiet Atmosphere - Maintain a quiet atmosphere on the bus.

Etiquette :

- Phone Usage - Keep phone conversations to a minimum and set phones to silent mode.

- No Eating/Drinking - Eating and drinking are generally not allowed on trains or buses.

- Lost and Found - If you lose something on public transportation, contact the station's or company's lost and found office.



Car Rental Guidelines:

- Driving Side - Japan drives on the left side of the road.

- International Driving Permit (IDP) - Non-Japanese citizens are required to have an IDP along with their valid home country's driver's license.

- Seat Belts - It is mandatory for all passengers to wear seat belts.

- Speed Limits - Adhere to posted speed limits, which can vary based on the type of road.

- Drunk Driving - Strict penalties exist for drunk driving. The legal blood alcohol concentration (BAC) limit is very low.

- Parking - Follow parking regulations to avoid fines. Paid parking is common in urban areas.


Bicycle and Scooter Rental Regulations:

Bicycle Rentals :

- Traffic Rules - Bicycles must follow the same traffic rules as vehicles.

- Parking - Park bicycles in designated areas to avoid fines or having the bike removed.

- Lights and Bells - Ensure that bicycles have working lights and bells, especially when riding at night.

Scooter Rentals :

- License Requirement - A regular car license or a specific scooter license is required for scooter rentals.

- Helmets - Helmets are mandatory when riding scooters.

- Traffic Rules - Follow traffic rules and speed limits for scooters.

- Parking - Park scooters in designated areas.
