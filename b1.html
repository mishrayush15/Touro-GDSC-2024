Know About JAPAN’S
General Behavior and related laws

Language Tips and Common Phrases:

- Language : The official language is Japanese. While English is not as widely spoken as in some other countries,
you can often find English speakers in major cities and tourist areas.

- Common Phrases -
- Hello - こんにちは (Konnichiwa)
  - Thank you - ありがとう (Arigatou)
  - Excuse me/Sorry - すみません (Sumimasen)
  - Yes - はい (Hai)
  - No - いいえ (Iie)
  - Please - お願いします (Onegaishimasu)

- Politeness - Japanese culture places a strong emphasis on politeness. Adding "san" (e.g., "Mr." or "Ms.") after a name is a sign of respect.

- Bow - A slight bow is a common greeting and a sign of respect in Japan.





Internet and Telecommunication Services:

- Internet Access - Wi-Fi is widely available in hotels, cafes, and public spaces in major cities. Many accommodations also provide free Wi-Fi.

- SIM Cards and Pocket Wi-Fi - Visitors can purchase prepaid SIM cards or portable Wi-Fi devices at airports, electronics stores, or online.
These can provide internet connectivity throughout your stay.

- Mobile Network - Japan has a reliable and extensive mobile network. Major carriers include NTT Docomo, SoftBank, and KDDI au.

- International Roaming - Check with your mobile carrier for international roaming options and charges if you plan to use your home country's SIM card.







Emergency Contact Numbers:

- Emergency Services - Dial 119 for fire and ambulance services, and 110 for police. Operators may not always speak English, so it's helpful to know some basic Japanese phrases or have someone who can assist you.

- Embassy/Consulate - Know the contact information for your country's embassy or consulate in case of emergencies or if you lose your passport.

- Japan Visitor Hotline - 050-3816-2787 (Available 24/7 for tourist information and assistance in multiple languages).
